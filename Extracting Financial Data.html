Extracting Financial
Data

Student Expenses

Context

This project was created to observe and identify the type of expenses that required improved budgeting. Using python, tabular data from monthly PDF bank statements sent by TD Bank were extracted, transformed, and preprocessed into dataframes. The dataframes for each year were then exported and loaded to Microsoft Excel files- with sheets corresponding to each month- to further visualize and study expenses. Macros were created in Excel to automate these tasks. Finally, the data was summarized and evaluated by comparing the expenses of 2021 and 2022 in order to identify expenses that required improved budgeting.

Results*

During the preprocessing phase in Excel, expenses were categorized into seven categories: saving, leisure, giving, subscription, consumption, gas, and fees. Once completed, each month's expenses were automated and visualized as tables and plots as oberserved in the sample figures below:

Conclusion

Based on the comparison on categorical expenses, areas where improved budgeting would be required are leisure and consumption. Although most expenses have been from fees, as the student has graduated this year, fees are projected to significantly reduce as tuition and rent are no longer expenses for 2023.
